The Seed

18th August 2015 sevaconnect

“is there anything else I could do to help out? Maybe something like organizing a fundraiser or advertising your organization on social media or something?”
The lady sitting in front of me looked at me as if I had said something improbably absurd. “No, you can only volunteer to teach kids. These great ideas of yours, leave it to the staff here.” The delicate sunlight, coming from the partially curtained windows irradiated parts of the office, as I sat, seemingly unabashed by the remarks of the head of Ashray, a non-profit organization for underprivileged kids where I was going to volunteer. I wondered if I would be able to do it well. I am generally impatient by nature. And, I believe teaching little kids requires a substantially degree of patience. 

Nonetheless, I showed up the next day to begin teaching kids. I was paired with this cute, seven year old girl and was told to teach her math, one of my favorite subjects at school. So I went up to the girl and said, “you like math?” “no, I like playing.” “Well, from now, you are going to like math as well. Let’s begin”. I felt really up to this new challenge in front of me. Half an hour later, my enthusiasm had pretty much died. The first fifteen minutes had been spent trying to explain the concept of multiplication (which, I am pretty sure, went over her head), the other fifteen, chasing the obstinate girl around the campus for she just did not want to study. 

After three days of volunteering, and trying out three different kids, I  guess I couldn’t see the value in it or may be I was too impatient. I wanted to make impact, huge impact. I really wished to do something which would benefit all of these children in a huge way, say, by getting them enrolled in good schools, hiring professionals to teach them here. So I quit teaching kids there. I realized I was too impatient for the job.So that was the beginning and the end of volunteering for me.
